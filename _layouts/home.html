Spectacular Trekking Adventures

Uttarakhand is a trekker’s paradise, offering a plethora of trekking routes that cater to every level of expertise. One of the most famous treks in the state is the Valley of Flowers, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This alpine meadow is a riot of colors during the monsoon season, with thousands of species of wildflowers in full bloom, and it’s surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

For the more adventurous, the Roopkund Trek takes you to the mysterious Skeleton Lake, which remains frozen for most of the year. The trek offers a unique combination of natural beauty and a chilling glimpse into the past, as the lake is said to hold the remains of several hundred people who died in an ancient event.

A trek to the Milam Glacier takes you to the heart of the Kumaon Himalayas, where you’ll pass through picturesque villages, dense forests, and, finally, reach the massive glacier. This trek offers a deep immersion into the culture of the region, as you meet friendly locals and learn about their way of life.

Wildlife Encounters in the Himalayan Foothills

Uttarakhand is home to some of India’s most remarkable wildlife, and its national parks and sanctuaries offer an opportunity for wildlife enthusiasts to observe diverse species in their natural habitat. Jim Corbett National Park, one of the oldest and most renowned wildlife reserves in the country, is famous for its population of Bengal tigers. A jungle safari here might reward you with sightings of these magnificent creatures, along with leopards, elephants, and a rich avian population.

Rajaji National Park, located not far from Haridwar and Rishikesh, is a haven for nature lovers. It boasts a variety of wildlife, including elephants, leopards, and over 300 species of birds. The park is home to the gharial, a critically endangered crocodilian species found in the Ganges and Yamuna rivers.

Hill Stations for Serene Getaways

Uttarakhand is graced with some of the most picturesque hill stations in India. Nainital, also known as the “City of Lakes,” is nestled around the shimmering Naini Lake and is surrounded by emerald hills. It’s a popular destination for honeymooners and families alike.

Mussorie, often referred to as the “Queen of Hills,” is known for its lush landscapes, pleasant climate, and enchanting views of the Himalayas. A visit to Mall Road and the famous Kempty Falls is a must when in Mussoorie.

Ranikhet, another serene hill station, offers solitude amidst its pine forests and pristine beauty. The Chaubatia Gardens and the Jhula Devi Temple are some of the attractions in this quiet town.

Auli, situated at a higher altitude, is renowned for its stunning vistas of snow-covered peaks. It’s a popular destination for skiing enthusiasts during the winter months.

One of the best destinations is Haridwar, located on the right bank of the Ganga and included in the seven sacred cities of India. This is also the best place to access the Chota Char Dham, a Hindu spiritual tour that includes four major pilgrimages. The city also has beautiful temples that can be visited and hosts the Kumbh Mela, a holy event held once every twelve years and gathering millions of pilgrims. Before planning a tour, every visitor has many questions in their minds.
